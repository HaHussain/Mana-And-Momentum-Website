Travel is the process of going to another [[Locations|Location]].

Travel out into the wilds is expected to occur on a hex-map while travel in urban centres can be handled without.
For smaller local maps, 1 hex represents a distance of 5 or 10km, where each hex is 1 or 2 hours of travel at standard speed respectively.
For larger maps, 1 hex represents 40km, where each hex is 1 day of travel at standard speed.

# Travelling Extra
Every 7m of Speed (Rounded Down), the [[Fortress]] moves 5km per hour (1 clock tick on an 8-[[Clock]] on a large map).
%% 7m/10s (SPEED) / 10 (Convert m/10s to m/s) * 2 (2 actions per turn) * 3.6 (m/s to km/h) = 5.04km/h %%

For every extra 2m of Speed the [[Fortress]] has, it moves an additional 10km per day (2 clock ticks on an 8-[[Clock]] on a large map)

When a travelling [[Clock]] is started, the players only move an extra hex after the clock completes and all travel clocks reset on reaching a [[Locations|Location]].

# Travelling Duties

When exploring out in the wild, there are many important tasks that must be handled to not die in the wilds.

Navigating - The process of ensuring you're on track to your destination and knowing where you are.
Scouting - Discovering the area around you.
Foraging - Looking for food and water
Handling* - Managing the animals and driving of the [[Fortress]].

## Navigating
Navigating helps ensure the party is on track and heading in the correct direction.
%%The Purpose of navigating is to ensure that it makes sense that mountains block travel, and people use roads, making journeying without following roads and markers more difficult. Players should feel like heading away from the roads innovative but scarier.%%
Navigation is an [[Awareness]] or [[Intelligence]] (Navigator's tools) roll, that can get more difficult if the terrain blocks line of sight or has no landmarks.
Success on a Navigation roll means the party remains on track, successfully moving to the next area.
Failure on the Navigation roll means the party begins to go astray, going to one of the two adjacent hexes instead of the intended one.
Failure by # means heading to any random hex.

## Scouting
Scouting helps discover the interesting things around the party as they travel and stops the dangerous things from attacking the party as they travel.
%%Scouting gives the party the opportunity to discover things about the world and its culture as well as subplots. Monsters aim to make the outskirts feel populated with living breathing beasts, many of which are dangerous.
In general when the party sets out on a journey, you can decide is this a moment for attrition or tension.
If the players are heading from one safe location to another, there is generally no point for attrition unless it is a long journey, where the likelihood of survival is in question.

In a scene where the goal is attrition - the goal is to have cool and interesting scenarios that drain the player's resources. And an interesting fight, like an ambush or some other people being attacked creates the opportunity of gaining some lore for their actions.
You can start this combat at any time, but do note, too much will slow down the pace of the story and cause disinterest so do this when it serves the story.

In a scene where the goal is tension - the goal is to immediately have a cool scene where there is a chance of failure.%%
Scouting is an [[Awareness]] roll, that can get more difficult if the terrain block line of sight.
